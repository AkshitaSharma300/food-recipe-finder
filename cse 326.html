Strawberry Custard

In a bowl, mix 2 tablespoons of custard powder with 1/4 cup cold milk until smooth. In a saucepan, heat 2 cups of milk and 1/4 cup sugar over medium heat, stirring occasionally. Once the milk starts to simmer, gradually add the custard mixture while stirring continuously to avoid lumps. Cook for 2-3 minutes until the custard thickens, then remove from heat and let it cool. Once cool, stir in 1 cup of chopped strawberries. Chill in the refrigerator for an hour, and serve topped with more fresh strawberries or whipped cream

Shakshuka

To make shakshuka, heat 2 tbsp olive oil in a skillet and sauté 1 diced onion, 1 bell pepper, and 3 minced garlic cloves until soft. Stir in 1 tsp cumin, 1 tsp paprika, ½ tsp chili powder, salt, and pepper, cooking for a minute. Add a 14-oz can of crushed tomatoes (or 3-4 fresh tomatoes, chopped) and simmer for 10-15 minutes until thickened. Make small wells in the sauce and crack 4-6 eggs into them, cover the pan, and cook on low until the egg whites are set but yolks remain runny (5-7 minutes). Garnish with chopped parsley and serve with crusty bread.

Grilled Kebabs

To make grilled kebabs, marinate 1 lb of cubed meat (like chicken, beef, or lamb) in a mixture of 1/4 cup olive oil, 2 tbsp lemon juice, 3 minced garlic cloves, 1 tsp cumin, 1 tsp paprika, salt, pepper, and chopped herbs like parsley or oregano for at least 1-2 hours. Thread the marinated meat onto skewers, alternating with vegetables such as bell peppers, onions, and zucchini. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat and cook the kebabs for 10-15 minutes, turning occasionally, until the meat is cooked through and slightly charred. Serve hot with pita bread, yogurt sauce, or a fresh salad.

Chapati

In a bowl, mix 2 cups of whole wheat flour with a pinch of salt. Gradually add 3/4 cup water and knead into a soft, smooth dough. Cover and let it rest for 20-30 minutes. Divide the dough into equal portions and roll each portion into a ball. Dust with flour and roll out into thin circles about 6-8 inches in diameter. Heat a tawa or skillet over medium heat. Cook the chapati on one side until bubbles appear, flip and cook the other side until light brown spots form. Flip once more, pressing gently with a spatula to puff it up. Serve warm with curry or vegetables.

Chhole Bhature

For chhole, soak 1 cup chickpeas overnight, then boil with salt and a pinch of baking soda until soft. In a pan, heat 2 tbsp oil, sauté 1 tsp cumin seeds, 2 chopped onions, and 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste until golden. Add 2 chopped tomatoes, 1 tsp turmeric, 2 tsp chhole masala, 1 tsp cumin powder, 1 tsp red chili powder, and salt; cook until oil separates. Mix in boiled chickpeas with 1/2 cup water and simmer for 10-15 minutes. For bhature, mix 2 cups all-purpose flour, 2 tbsp semolina, 1 tsp sugar, 1/2 tsp salt, 1/4 tsp baking powder, and 1/4 cup yogurt, then knead into a soft dough and rest for 1 hour. Roll into circles and deep-fry until puffed and golden. Serve hot with the prepared chhole.

Croissant

To make croissants, start by mixing 4 cups of all-purpose flour, 1/4 cup sugar, 2 teaspoons salt, and 2 teaspoons instant yeast in a bowl. In a separate bowl, combine 1 cup of warm milk and 1/2 cup of melted butter. Gradually add this mixture to the dry ingredients, mixing until a dough forms. Knead for about 5 minutes until smooth, then let it rise for 1-2 hours until doubled in size. Roll the dough into a rectangle and place 1 cup of cold, unsalted butter (cut into slabs) in the center. Fold the dough over the butter, seal the edges, and roll it out into a larger rectangle. Fold it into thirds like a letter, then refrigerate for 30 minutes. Repeat this rolling and folding process two more times. After the final fold, roll the dough out to about 1/4-inch thickness and cut it into triangles. Roll each triangle from the base to the tip to form a crescent shape. Place the croissants on a baking sheet, cover, and let them rise for about 1 hour. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C) and bake for 15-20 minutes until golden brown. Enjoy your flaky, buttery croissants warm!

Curry

To make a basic curry, heat 2-3 tablespoons of oil in a pan over medium heat. Add 1 chopped onion and sauté until golden brown. Stir in 1 tablespoon of ginger-garlic paste and cook for another minute. Add 2 chopped tomatoes, 1 teaspoon turmeric, 1 teaspoon cumin powder, 1 teaspoon coriander powder, and salt to taste. Cook until the tomatoes are soft and the oil starts to separate. If using meat (like chicken or beef), add it now and cook until browned. For a vegetarian option, add 2 cups of your favorite vegetables (like potatoes, peas, or bell peppers) and sauté for a few minutes. Pour in 1 cup of water or coconut milk, cover, and simmer for 20-25 minutes until the meat is tender or the vegetables are cooked through. Finish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with rice or naan.

Dahi Bhalla

To make dahi bhalla, soak 1 cup of urad dal (split black gram) in water for 4-5 hours, then drain and blend it into a smooth batter with a little water. Whisk in 1/2 tsp salt and 1/2 tsp cumin powder. Heat oil in a pan and drop spoonfuls of the batter to fry until golden brown; drain on paper towels. For the yogurt sauce, whisk together 2 cups of plain yogurt with a pinch of salt and sugar to taste. Soak the fried bhallas in warm water for about 15-20 minutes, then gently squeeze out excess water. Place the bhallas on a serving plate, drizzle with the yogurt, and top with tamarind chutney, green chutney, roasted cumin powder, and chopped coriander. Serve chilled for a refreshing snack!

Dal Makhani

To make dal makhani, start by soaking 1 cup of black urad dal (whole black gram) and 1/4 cup of kidney beans overnight. Drain and rinse, then pressure cook them with 4 cups of water, 1/2 tsp turmeric, and salt for about 15-20 minutes until soft. In a separate pan, heat 2-3 tablespoons of butter and 1 tablespoon of oil. Add 1 chopped onion and sauté until golden brown. Stir in 1 tablespoon of ginger-garlic paste, followed by 2 chopped tomatoes, 1 teaspoon cumin powder, 1 teaspoon coriander powder, and 1 teaspoon garam masala. Cook until the tomatoes are soft. Add the cooked dal and beans, along with 1 cup of cream (or milk for a lighter version) and simmer on low heat for 30-40 minutes, stirring occasionally. Adjust the seasoning as needed, and garnish with fresh coriander and a dollop of butter before serving with naan or rice.

Dhokhla

To make dhokla, mix 1 cup of gram flour (besan) with 1 teaspoon of ginger paste, 1 teaspoon of green chili paste, 1 teaspoon of sugar, 1 teaspoon of salt, and 1/2 teaspoon of turmeric powder in a bowl. Gradually add about 1 cup of water to form a smooth batter. Stir in 1 teaspoon of lemon juice and 1 teaspoon of Eno fruit salt or baking soda just before steaming. Pour the batter into a greased steaming tray and steam for about 15-20 minutes until a toothpick comes out clean. Once cooked, let it cool for a few minutes, then cut into squares. For the tempering, heat 2 tablespoons of oil, add 1 teaspoon of mustard seeds, and 1-2 green chilies, and let them crackle. Pour this tempering over the dhokla, garnish with chopped coriander, and serve with green chutney. Enjoy your fluffy, savory dhokla as a snack or breakfast!

Pour a ladleful of batter onto the skillet and spread it in a circular motion to form a thin layer. Drizzle a little oil around the edges and cook for 2-3 minutes until the bottom is golden brown and crispy. Flip and cook for another minute, if desired. Serve hot with coconut chutney and sambar for a delicious meal!

Gulab Jamun

To make gulab jamun, start by mixing 1 cup of milk powder, 1/4 cup of all-purpose flour, and 1/4 teaspoon of baking soda in a bowl. Add 2 tablespoons of ghee (clarified butter) and gradually add milk until a soft dough forms. Let the dough rest for about 15 minutes. Meanwhile, prepare the sugar syrup by boiling 1 cup of sugar and 1 cup of water with a few drops of lemon juice and a pinch of cardamom powder until the sugar dissolves. Remove from heat and let it cool. After resting, divide the dough into small balls (about the size of a marble). Heat oil or ghee in a deep frying pan over medium heat and fry the balls until golden brown, ensuring they are cooked through. Once fried, soak the hot gulab jamuns in the warm sugar syrup for at least 30 minutes. Serve warm or at room temperature, garnished with chopped pistachios or almonds if desired!

Idli

To make idli, start by soaking 1 cup of rice and 1/4 cup of urad dal (split black gram) in water for 6-8 hours or overnight. Drain and blend the soaked ingredients with a little water until you achieve a smooth batter. Transfer the batter to a bowl, cover it, and let it ferment in a warm place for 8-12 hours until it doubles in size and becomes slightly bubbly. Once fermented, add salt to taste and mix gently. Prepare an idli steamer or a large pot with water and bring it to a boil. Grease the idli molds with oil and pour the batter into each mold, filling them about 3/4 full. Place the molds in the steamer and steam for 10-15 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the idlis comes out clean. Remove the molds and let them cool for a minute before gently taking out the idlis. Serve hot with coconut chutney and sambar for a delicious breakfast or snack!

Kachori

To make kachori, start by preparing the filling. Heat 2 tablespoons of oil in a pan and add 1 teaspoon of cumin seeds. Place a spoonful of the filling in the center, fold the edges over, and seal tightly. Flatten gently to form a disc. Heat oil in a deep frying pan and fry the kachoris on medium heat until golden brown and crispy. Serve hot with tamarind chutney or yogurt for a delicious snack!

Pizza

To make pizza, start by mixing 2 cups of all-purpose flour, 1 teaspoon of instant yeast, 1 teaspoon of sugar, and 1/2 teaspoon of salt in a bowl, then gradually add 3/4 cup of warm water and knead until smooth; cover and let it rise in a warm place for about 1 hour until doubled in size. Meanwhile, prepare the pizza sauce by sautéing 1 clove of minced garlic in 1 tablespoon of olive oil, adding 1 can of crushed tomatoes (about 400 grams), 1 teaspoon of dried oregano, salt, and pepper, and simmering for 15 minutes. rasmalai

To make rasmalai, start by preparing chenna (fresh cheese) by boiling 1 liter of milk and curdling it with lemon juice or vinegar; strain the curds through a muslin cloth and rinse with cold water to remove the acidic taste. Once cool, knead the chenna until smooth and form small, flat discs. In a pot, boil 4 cups of water with 1 cup of sugar to create a syrup, then gently add the chenna discs and cook for about 10 minutes until they double in size. For the ras, boil another 1 liter of milk in a saucepan, reducing it to half while stirring continuously, then add 1/4 cup of sugar, 1/4 teaspoon of cardamom powder, and a few saffron strands for flavor. Once thickened, remove from heat, let it cool, and add the cooked chenna discs to the milk, soaking for at least 2 hours in the refrigerator. Serve chilled, garnished with slivers of pistachio or almonds, for a delicious and refreshing dessert!

Red Velvet Cake

To make a red velvet cake, preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease two 9-inch round cake pans. In a bowl, mix 2 ½ cups of all-purpose flour, 1 ½ cups of sugar, 1 teaspoon of baking soda, 1 teaspoon of salt, and 1 teaspoon of cocoa powder. In another bowl, combine 1 cup of vegetable oil, 1 cup of buttermilk, 2 large eggs, 2 tablespoons of red food coloring, 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract, and 1 teaspoon of white vinegar. Gradually add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients, mixing until just combined. Divide the batter evenly between the prepared pans and bake for 25-30 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean. While the cakes cool, prepare the cream cheese frosting by beating together 8 oz of cream cheese, ½ cup of unsalted butter, 4 cups of powdered sugar, and 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract until smooth and fluffy. Once the cakes are completely cool, layer them with the cream cheese frosting between the layers and on top. Decorate as desired and enjoy your delicious red velvet cake!

Samosa

To make samosas, start by preparing the dough by mixing 2 cups of all-purpose flour with 1/2 teaspoon of salt and 4 tablespoons of oil or ghee, then gradually add enough water to form a firm dough; cover and let it rest for about 30 minutes. For the filling, boil 3 medium-sized potatoes and mash them, then mix with 1/2 cup of green peas, 1 teaspoon of cumin seeds, 1 teaspoon of garam masala, 1 teaspoon of coriander powder, and salt to taste, along with chopped green chilies and cilantro for extra flavor. Roll the dough into small balls and flatten each ball into a thin circle, then cut it in half to form two semi-circles. Take one semi-circle, fold it into a cone shape, seal the edge with a little water, and fill it with the potato filling before sealing the open edge. Deep-fry the samosas in hot oil until golden brown and crispy, then drain on paper towels. Serve hot with mint chutney or tamarind sauce for a delicious snack!

Sandwich

To make a sandwich, start by choosing your bread, such as whole grain, white, or sourdough, and lightly toasting two slices if desired. Spread a layer of mayonnaise or butter on one side of each slice, then layer your favorite fillings, like sliced deli meats (ham, turkey, or chicken), cheese (cheddar, Swiss, or mozzarella), and fresh vegetables (lettuce, tomatoes, cucumbers, and onions). For added flavor, include condiments such as mustard, pesto, or hummus. If you like, add avocado or sliced pickles for extra texture. Top with the second slice of bread, cut the sandwich diagonally or in half, and serve with chips, a pickle, or a side salad for a quick and satisfying meal!

Soup

To make a delicious soup, start by heating 2 tablespoons of olive oil in a large pot over medium heat and sautéing 1 chopped onion, 2 minced garlic cloves, and 1 diced carrot until they soften, about 5 minutes. Add 4 cups of vegetable or chicken broth and bring to a boil. Incorporate your choice of vegetables, such as diced potatoes, green beans, or spinach, and season with salt, pepper, and herbs like thyme or basil. For added richness, include 1 can of diced tomatoes or 1 cup of cooked beans. Let the soup simmer for 20-30 minutes until the vegetables are tender. Blend with an immersion blender for a creamy texture or leave it chunky for a hearty finish. Serve hot, garnished with fresh herbs or a sprinkle of grated cheese for a comforting meal!

South Indian Combo

To prepare a South Indian combo, start by making idli by soaking 1 cup of urad dal and 2 cups of idli rice overnight, then grinding them separately into a smooth batter and mixing them together; let it ferment for about 8 hours before steaming in idli molds for 10-15 minutes. Alongside, prepare sambar by boiling chopped vegetables like carrots, beans, and potatoes with 1/2 cup of toor dal, adding 2 tablespoons of sambar powder, tamarind paste, and salt; simmer until the vegetables are tender. For coconut chutney, blend 1 cup of grated coconut, 2 green chilies, a small piece of ginger, and salt with some water until smooth, then temper with mustard seeds and curry leaves in oil. Serve the fluffy idlis with hot sambar and coconut chutney, and complement the meal with a glass of buttermilk for a refreshing touch, making for a complete and satisfying South Indian feast!

Soya Chaap

To make soya chaap, start by marinating 500 grams of soya chaap (soya protein sticks) in a mixture of 1 cup of yogurt, 2 tablespoons of ginger-garlic paste, 2 tablespoons of lemon juice, 1 teaspoon of red chili powder, 1 teaspoon of turmeric powder, 1 teaspoon of garam masala, and salt to taste; let it marinate for at least 1 hour, preferably overnight in the refrigerator. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C) or prepare a grill. Thread the marinated soya chaap onto skewers, brushing them lightly with oil. Bake or grill for about 20-25 minutes, turning occasionally, until they are golden brown and slightly charred. Meanwhile, prepare a mint chutney by blending fresh mint leaves, coriander, green chilies, lemon juice, and salt. Serve the hot, smoky soya chaap with the mint chutney and sliced onions for a delicious and protein-rich appetizer or main dish!

Spinach

To prepare a simple and delicious spinach dish, start by washing and roughly chopping 4 cups of fresh spinach leaves. In a large pan, heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil or butter over medium heat and add 1 chopped onion and 2 minced garlic cloves; sauté until the onion is translucent. Add the chopped spinach to the pan, along with a pinch of salt and pepper, and cook for about 3-5 minutes until the spinach wilts down. For added flavor, you can stir in a squeeze of lemon juice or a sprinkle of red chili flakes. Once cooked, remove from heat and serve the sautéed spinach as a healthy side dish or incorporate it into pasta, omelets, or sandwiches for an extra nutrient boost!

Subway

To make a Subway-style sandwich at home, start with a fresh sub roll, like Italian or whole grain, and slice it lengthwise without cutting all the way through to create a hinge. Spread your choice of condiments, such as mayonnaise, mustard, or a vinaigrette, on the inside, then layer in your favorite deli meats like turkey, ham, or salami, followed by slices of cheese such as cheddar, provolone, or Swiss. Add fresh vegetables like lettuce, tomatoes, cucumbers, onions, and bell peppers for crunch, and consider including jalapeños or pickles for an extra kick. Slice it in half and serve with a side of chips or a pickle for a satisfying and customizable meal that captures the essence of a Subway sandwich!

Tea

To make a comforting cup of tea, start by boiling 1 cup of water in a saucepan or kettle. Once the water reaches a rolling boil, add 1-2 teaspoons of loose tea leaves or one tea bag of your choice (black, green, or herbal). Allow it to steep for about 3-5 minutes, depending on how strong you like your tea. Vada Pav

To make vada pav, start by preparing the vada (spiced potato filling) by boiling and mashing 3 medium-sized potatoes, then heating 2 tablespoons of oil in a pan to add 1 teaspoon each of mustard seeds and cumin seeds, along with a pinch of turmeric powder; once they splutter, mix in the mashed potatoes, 1 chopped green chili, and salt to taste, along with freshly chopped coriander leaves, and let the mixture cool before shaping it into small balls. For the batter, combine 1 cup of besan (gram flour) with a pinch of turmeric, red chili powder, and salt, then add water to create a smooth batter. Heat oil in a deep pan for frying, dip each potato ball into the batter, and fry until golden brown. For the pav (bread), slice each bun and lightly toast it with butter on a hot pan. Assemble the vada pav by placing a vada between the pav and serve with green chutney, tamarind chutney, and fried green chilies for a delicious Indian street food experience!

Vegetable Biryani

To make vegetable biryani, start by soaking 1 ½ cups of basmati rice in water for about 30 minutes, then drain. In a large pot, heat 3 tablespoons of oil or ghee, and add whole spices such as 1 bay leaf, 4-5 cloves, 2-3 green cardamom pods, and a small cinnamon stick, sautéing until fragrant. Add 1 chopped onion and sauté until golden brown, then stir in 1 tablespoon of ginger-garlic paste and 1-2 chopped green chilies, cooking for another minute. Incorporate 2 cups of mixed vegetables like carrots, peas, and beans, and sauté for 5 minutes. Next, add the drained rice, 3 cups of water, and season with salt to taste, along with 1 teaspoon of biryani masala or garam masala. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low, cover, and cook for about 15-20 minutes until the rice is tender and the water is absorbed. Fluff the biryani with a fork, garnish with chopped cilantro and mint leaves, and serve hot with raita or salad for a flavorful and aromatic meal!

Waffles

To make delicious waffles, start by preheating your waffle iron. In a bowl, mix together 2 cups of all-purpose flour, 2 tablespoons of sugar, 1 tablespoon of baking powder, and a pinch of salt. In another bowl, whisk together 2 large eggs, 1 ¾ cups of milk, and ½ cup of melted butter or vegetable oil until well combined. Gradually pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients, stirring until just combined (some lumps are okay). Lightly grease the waffle iron with cooking spray or butter, then pour the batter onto the hot iron, filling it about two-thirds full. Close the waffle iron and cook according to the manufacturer's instructions, usually around 4-5 minutes, until the waffles are golden brown and crispy. Serve the waffles warm, topped with maple syrup, fresh fruits, whipped cream, or your favorite toppings for a delightful breakfast or brunch treat!

Cheese Chilly

To make cheese chili, start by cutting 250 grams of paneer (Indian cottage cheese) into bite-sized cubes and preparing a batter by mixing 1/2 cup of all-purpose flour with 1/4 cup of cornflour, 1 teaspoon of red chili powder, and a pinch of salt. Dip the paneer cubes into the batter, ensuring they are well coated, and deep-fry them in hot oil until golden and crispy. In another pan, heat 2 tablespoons of oil and sauté 1 chopped onion and 2-3 chopped green chilies until the onion becomes translucent. Add 1 chopped bell pepper and sauté for another minute, then mix in 2 tablespoons of soy sauce, 1 tablespoon of tomato ketchup, and 1 teaspoon of chili sauce. Toss the fried paneer cubes into the pan, gently coating them with the sauce and vegetables, and cook for a couple of minutes to heat through. Garnish with chopped green onions and serve hot as a delicious appetizer or snack!
